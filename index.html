About Me

Hello! I want to have a positive impact on the world by helping prevent an AI-related global catastrophe. I have an intuition that I would be best suited for an information security role at a company working on AI safety. I spent the last two and a half years traveling the world, the first year while working remotely. After leaving Stripe, I needed time to figure out what I wanted to do with the rest of my life. I’ve stood on top of high mountains, walked in the depths of lonely forests, sat in the scorching sun of vast deserts, and admired the vibrant colors of coral reefs, each time in awe of the universe. I’ve been invited into people’s homes without any thought of payment or even being able to speak their language. I’ve seen that we, no matter how different we seem from each other, we’re all basically the same. I’ve seen the kindness in human nature, the diversity yet similarity of our experiences, and I know very well what we have to lose. These two and a half years weren't a waste of time. I've learned to be adaptable, resourceful, persistent, and an efficient yet respectful communicator. I couldn't have gotten so good these soft skills while working at a company. Most of all, I've gotten a sense of what gives me meaning in life and what I want to live for.

My Stuff

HollowOS

A homemade operating system. Created mainly for fun and learning purposes. Current goal is to create a simple, monolithic kernel that features preemptive multitasking, along with a barebones text user interface, though this is subject to change.

Grocery Split

A simple web server written in Go that allows for uneven bill splitting, originally created to split the grocery bill. Parses generic HTML containing a list of items with prices and allows users to select which items they want to pay for.

Anime Calendar

A Japanese television animation calendar desktop application. It is written in Node.js. Pulls information from the AniList API, organizes it based on air time. It uses the Electron framework for the GUI.

Adventure Land

Adventure Land is a browser-based MMORPG that has an interesting feature: players are allowed and encouraged to automate every action by writing JavaScript. The game gets surprisingly deep, and it takes considerable thought to design a robust system that cannot be exploited by other human players.
